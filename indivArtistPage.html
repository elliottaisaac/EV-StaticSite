ADELA GOLDBARD
Adela Goldbard is an interdisciplinary artist and educator who believes in the potential of art to generate critical thinking and social transformation. Her work questions the politics of memory by suspecting power relations and social constructs behind official history, archeological preservation, patriotism, state-sanctioned celebrations, and mass media. She is especially interested in how destruction can become a ritual, a statement, a metaphor, a way of remembering and a form of disobedience.

The Last Judgment:

Ritual Destruction as Aesthetic Disobedience (2019)

Adela’s project, The Last Judgment, is a co-created, participatory artwork that will culminate in a pyrotechnic performance featuring the ritual destruction of large-scale sculptures built for a series of narrative vignettes co-written and performed by Little Village residents. A multilingual (Spanish, English, Náhuatl) script based on environmental justice, migration, incarceration and gentrification concerns has been developed collaboratively with Little Village residents. Local teaching artists William Estrada, Teresa Magaña, Juan-Carlos Perez, Silvia Inés Gonzalez and art teacher Sue Voss facilitated community art workshops to collect local histories. Corazon de Valor y Fortaleza (Pilsen) worked with Telpochcalli Community Education Project to develop a script and the Artsumex Collective (Tultepec, México) worked with community members to build the effigies. Finally, Mariachi Pantera of Little Village Academy collaborated on the soundtrack for the performance, which will take in Spring of 2020.
